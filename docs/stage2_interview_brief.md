# Stage 2 Interview Brief

## Opening

Trust Layer for Inclusive Fintech helps a live fintech turn women-customer trust
from a vague aspiration into an operating metric. It instruments high-risk
journey moments, adds safeguards, and reports whether women customers experience
clearer terms, safer transactions, better recourse, and stronger usage.

## Five Proof Points

1. The solution is embedded in live fintech journeys through the partner.
2. Trust signals are operational: fee clarity, fraud warning, consent, complaint resolution, affordability, bias review, collections safeguards.
3. Metrics are gender-disaggregated and tied to outcomes.
4. The product changes from data, not assumptions.
5. Harm reduction improves the business through retention, repeat usage, lower complaints, and lower loss.

## Expected Questions

**How do you know this is not gender-washing?**  
Because readiness requires women-customer metrics, a GDD-informed change, and a measured outcome. The package blocks final submit until partner live data replaces synthetic samples.

**What makes this different from fraud prevention?**  
Fraud is one trust barrier. The trust layer covers the full journey: terms, consent, scam risk, complaints, repayment stress, collections, and fair access.

**How do you protect privacy?**  
Use pseudonymous IDs, aggregate reporting, data minimization, consent copy, retention boundaries, and partner-approved public evidence.

**How does this scale?**  
The V engine accepts event exports from existing fintech systems and outputs a standard trust/GDD report. The Vue cockpit lets product, risk, CX, and compliance teams review the same evidence.

**Why will the business care?**  
Low trust drives disengagement, complaints, fraud exposure, and repayment stress. Better trust improves completion, retention, repeat usage, referrals, and operational risk.
