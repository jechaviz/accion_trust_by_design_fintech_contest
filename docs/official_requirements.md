## Dates

- Applications opened: 2026-04-20.
- Stage 1 deadline: 2026-06-05 23:59 EDT, because the official rules allow extension from 2026-05-22 when fewer than 75 submissions are received.
- Stage 2 finalist notification: around the end of Stage 1, with interview between Stage 1 close and final winner selection.
- Summit attendance for winners: 2026-09-02 in London.

## Entrant Eligibility

- Entrant is a fintech or financial-services organization, not an individual project.
- Applicant must be at least 18 or local age of majority and must be legally authorized to bind the entrant.
- Entrant and applicant must not be in or subject to comprehensively sanctioned OFAC jurisdictions or lists.
- Eligible entrants include digital banks, MFIs, fintech lenders, neobanks, mobile money providers, MNOs, PSPs, digital wallets, insurtechs, and digital insurance providers.
- Strong entrants are typically post-revenue, operating in-market, serving women customers, collecting and using gender-disaggregated data, and demonstrating measurable trust, safety, or harm-reduction outcomes.

## Submission Eligibility

- Submission must be in English.
- One entry per entrant.
- Submission must avoid private, confidential, obscene, infringing, discriminatory, or misleading material.
- Disqualification risks include gender-washing, unclear privacy or consent, excessive data use, weak consumer safeguards, inability to report GDD, unsupported impact claims, concept-only posture, lack of operational capacity, weak controls, or weak complaint handling.

## Stage 1 Judging Weights

- Traction and market validation: 30%.
- Harm reduction and trust signal: 25%.
- Use of gender-disaggregated data: 20%.
- Regulatory and compliance evidence: 15%.
- Clarity and coherence of product or solution: 10%.

## Stage 2 Themes

- Innovation in trust building.
- Impact for women customers.
- Intentionality in gender-responsive design, research, data use, consent, and privacy.
- Implementation readiness through live features, users reached, partnerships, adoption, and operational capacity.
